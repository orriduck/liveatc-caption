## Design Context

### Users
ADSBao is for aviation-curious users, developers, and airport watchers who want a fast, glanceable airport operations view. They are usually checking an airport by ICAO/IATA, reading live METAR context, and watching nearby ADS-B traffic on a map. The app should support short, repeated sessions where the map remains the primary surface and supporting information stays compact.

### Brand Personality
Industrial, precise, and cinematic. ADSBao should feel like a modern airport operations HUD rather than a generic dashboard. It can be dramatic and visually distinctive, but it should still communicate live aviation data with restraint and confidence.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a dark aviation radar aesthetic anchored by a full-screen map, warm ATC orange accents, frosted glass panels, and technical data typography. The interface should feel spatial: map first, overlay second, text last. Use big airport identity moments sparingly, especially the italic serif airport code, but keep utility labels quiet and compact.

### Visual Language
- Backgrounds should be near-black with subtle warm gradients, map texture, and restrained vignettes. Avoid flat black panels unless they are part of a deliberate contrast moment.
- Accent color is ADSBao orange (`#FF5A1F`). Use it for airport identity, active aviation signals, and selective emphasis, not for every label.
- Typography pairs a clean sans-serif for names and narrative text, `Instrument Serif` for expressive airport/IATA identity, and `JetBrains Mono` for METAR, coordinates, timestamps, and compact labels.
- Glassmorphism is allowed because this product overlays information on a map. Use it as a functional depth layer, not as decorative nested cards.
- Panels should be shallow, compact, and map-preserving. The airport map should remain visible and useful at all desktop widths.

### Layout Principles
- The map is the hero. Any card, label, or header must justify the amount of map area it covers.
- Use progressive disclosure by viewport width: remove secondary operational panels before shrinking the map into unusability.
- Avoid nested cards inside cards. Prefer dividers, key-value rows, compact typography, and whitespace.
- Separate navigation, identity, and data. Breadcrumbs should stay minimal, e.g. `ADSBao / {airport_name}`; airport identity should live in its own lockup below it.
- Keep live operational data near the bottom overlay layer so the center of the map remains readable.

### Component Guidelines
- Header: use a quiet breadcrumb, then a distinct airport identity lockup. Do not mix country, ICAO, city, and title into one breadcrumb row.
- Airport title: let the airport code act as a visual anchor, but align it intentionally with the airport name. Do not let the title appear attached to the breadcrumb.
- Weather/METAR: raw METAR may use bold monospace, but parsed weather should be compact key-value text, not a grid of inner cards.
- Traffic: the dashboard card should summarize map-visible aircraft counts; individual aircraft details belong on the map, not in a repeated list inside the card.
- Wiki: use it as the final fallback/info panel. It can be more text-heavy than other panels but must be scroll-contained.

### Accessibility And Interaction
- Preserve strong contrast on dark backgrounds; dim labels are acceptable only when paired with high-contrast values.
- Support reduced motion by keeping animations subtle and nonessential.
- Keep tap targets usable on mobile even when panels are hidden progressively.
- Do not rely on color alone for aircraft state; when practical, pair color with labels or shape/position cues.

### Anti-Patterns
- Do not build generic analytics dashboards with many equal cards.
- Do not use busy breadcrumbs or redundant metadata rows.
- Do not put decorative nested glass cards inside larger glass cards.
- Do not let overlays obscure the aircraft cluster or central airport map.
- Do not overuse neon/cyberpunk effects; ADSBao should feel operational, not arcade-like.
